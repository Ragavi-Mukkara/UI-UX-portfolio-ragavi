Case study

Breast Cancer Awareness Webpage

A health awareness webpage that communicates sensitive information clearly, compassionately, and accessibly for a broad audience.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Focus

Web UX, content design, accessibility

Tools

Figma

Prototype

View Figma prototype →

Problem

Many health awareness websites use dense text, poor readability, and overwhelming medical terminology, which discourages users from engaging with important content.

Users need information that feels approachable, understandable, and emotionally respectful.

Design goals

  • Present information in a clear, readable format.
  • Maintain a calm, empathetic visual tone.
  • Improve accessibility through typography and contrast.
  • Structure content for easy scanning.

Content flow

  • Introduction
  • Awareness information
  • Symptoms & prevention
  • Resources & support

This structure helps visitors progressively understand, identify, and seek support.

Key UX decisions

Content‑first design

  • Prioritized readability over decoration.
  • Used spacing and headings to break content into digestible sections.

Accessible typography

  • High‑contrast text and comfortable line spacing.
  • Clear font hierarchy for headings, subheadings, and body text.

Emotionally sensitive visuals

  • Soft colours and minimal distractions.
  • Visual language that feels calm and supportive.

Key screens

UX improvements

Aspect Improvement
Readability Improved through typography and spacing.
Content flow Structured for easier understanding.
Accessibility Better contrast and font choices.
Emotional tone Supportive and non‑overwhelming.

Impact & takeaways

This project strengthened my understanding of designing with empathy in sensitive domains like healthcare.

It showed how UX choices around content, layout, and tone directly influence emotional comfort and trust.
