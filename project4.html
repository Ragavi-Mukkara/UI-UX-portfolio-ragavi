Case study

Coffee & Desserts Ordering Webpage

An online ordering experience designed to encourage smooth browsing and reduce drop‑offs during checkout for coffee and dessert orders.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Focus

Web UI/UX, e‑commerce flow, conversion

Tools

Figma

Prototype

View Figma prototype →

Problem

Users often abandon food ordering websites because of unclear navigation, confusing menus, and complicated checkout processes.

This leads to frustration, incomplete orders, and lost opportunities for the business.

Design goals

  • Create an engaging yet simple browsing experience.
  • Highlight products clearly with strong visual hierarchy.
  • Guide users smoothly from menu to checkout.
  • Reduce friction during ordering and payment.

User flow

  • Menu browsing → Item selection → Cart → Checkout

This flow mirrors how users naturally discover items, decide, and complete an order.

Key UX decisions

Strong visual hierarchy

  • Product cards with clear images, names, and prices.
  • Primary CTAs that visually stand out for quick action.

Simplified checkout

  • Reduced unnecessary steps and distractions.
  • Clear action buttons that guide users to completion.

Consistent layout

  • Predictable structure across pages to support usability.
  • Helps users move confidently through the ordering flow.

Key screens

UX improvements

Aspect Improvement
Navigation More intuitive and predictable.
Checkout Reduced confusion and friction.
Product clarity Improved through layout and spacing.
User flow Smooth transition from menu to payment.

Impact & takeaways

This project deepened my understanding of conversion‑focused design and how UI clarity and CTA placement influence user decisions.

It reinforced the importance of pairing clean visuals with a straightforward, low‑friction checkout flow.
